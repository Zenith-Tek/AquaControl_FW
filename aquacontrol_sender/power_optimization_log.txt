================================================================================
AQUACONTROL SENDER - POWER OPTIMIZATION LOG
================================================================================

This document tracks all power-saving techniques implemented in the AquaControl
Sender (ESP32-C3) firmware to date, along with estimated energy/current savings
at each stage.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PERIPHERAL ON-DEMAND POWER GATING (MOSFET CONTROL)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description:
  High-side P-channel MOSFETs gate power to the SX1278 (LoRa) and JSN-SR04T 
  (ultrasonic sensor) power rails via GPIO 9 and GPIO 10. When entering deep 
  sleep, they are driven HIGH (OFF) and held utilizing ESP32-C3 GPIO pad hold 
  functions (`gpio_hold_en()` and `gpio_deep_sleep_hold_en()`).
* Savings:
  - Deep Sleep Leakage Saved: ~5.0 mA to 8.0 mA continuous.
  - Explanation: Without power gating, the JSN-SR04T board draws 5-8 mA even 
    when idle. Holding its power gate off reduces this leakage to 0.
  - Impact: Extends standby battery life of a 3000mAh battery from ~18-24 days 
    to over 4 years.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SAFE-ADAPTIVE SLEEP STATE MACHINE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description:
  Dynamically scales the deep sleep duration depending on the state of the 
  system:
  - Active Filling (Pump ON, level <= 70%): Sleep 60 seconds.
  - Active Filling (Pump ON, level 70-80%): Sleep 30 seconds.
  - Active Filling (Pump ON, level > 80%): Sleep 15 seconds.
  - Idle (Pump OFF, level < 80%, Auto ON): Sleep 5 minutes (300s).
  - Idle (Pump OFF, level < 80%, Auto OFF): Sleep 15 minutes (900s).
  - Safety Ceiling (Pump OFF, level >= 80%): Sleep 2 minutes (120s).
  - Offline Mode (Unpaired, in light): Sleep 30 minutes (1800s).
  - Shipping Mode (Unpaired, in dark/box): Sleep 2 hours (7200s).
* Savings:
  - Active State Runtime Saved: 95% to 99.8% reduction.
  - Explanation: Scales wake cycle intervals depending on water filling speed
    to keep battery footprint minimal when static.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. CRITICAL BATTERY SHUTDOWN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description:
  If battery level drops below 10%, the firmware keeps the power gate to the 
  ultrasonic sensor OFF (skipping measurement) and goes into deep sleep for 
  12 hours. It wakes up only twice a day to transmit a low-battery emergency 
  beacon before sleeping again.
* Savings:
  - Remaining Capacity Preservation: ~99.9% reduction in remaining discharge.
  - Active time drops to ~20ms (LoRa only, no sensor delay).
  - Wakeups drop to 2 per day.
  - Daily active energy usage: 2 cycles * 200ms * 15mA = 0.0016 mAh/day.
  - Impact: Prevents cell degradation and extends depleted-battery beacon 
    lifetime from a few days to several months.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. COMPLETE WIFI DISABLEMENT (SDK & BUILD LEVEL)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description:
  Completely disabled the WiFi stack using `CONFIG_ESP_WIFI_ENABLED=n` in 
  `sdkconfig`/`sdkconfig.defaults` and removed `#include <esp_wifi.h>`.
* Savings:
  - Active Phase Energy Saved: ~10% to 20% reduction.
  - Peak Radio Current Saved: Prevents transient calibration/RX peaks of 84 mA 
    to 335 mA.
  - Memory/Flash Saved: Freed ~20-30 KB SRAM and reduced Flash size by ~100 KB+, 
    leading to faster boot-up times (saving ~50-150 ms of active CPU time per 
    boot).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. PHASE 1: CONFIGURATION, TIMING & GPIO OPTIMIZATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description:
  - Added dynamic compile-time macro logging override via `PRODUCTION_MODE`.
  - Downclocked CPU default frequency to 80 MHz in `sdkconfig.defaults`.
  - Reduced LoRa ACK receive wait timeout from 200 ms to 50 ms.
  - Reset and isolated unused GPIO 8 to `GPIO_FLOATING` state before deep sleep.
  - Reduced peripheral power rails stabilization delay from 50 ms to 20 ms.
* Savings:
  - Standby deep sleep current reduced from ~11.5 uA to **~11.0 uA**.
  - Active phase current reduced from ~50 mA to **~45 mA**.
  - Active duration reduced from ~500 ms to **~400 ms**.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. PHASE 2: ACTIVE MEASUREMENT DURATION REDUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description:
  - Reduced ultrasonic ping samples from 5 to 3 in `read_filtered_ultrasonic_distance()`.
  - Reduced ping spacing delay from 60 ms to 35 ms.
* Savings:
  - Active phase duration reduced from ~400 ms to **~200 ms**.
  - Active phase energy reduced to **9.0 mA-s** per cycle.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. PHASE 3: EVENT-BASED REPORTING (SMART WAKEUP)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description:
  - Implemented 2 consecutive reads change detection (4 cm hysteresis) and 2% battery shift check.
  - Implemented 2-hour RTC-based heartbeat using `gettimeofday()`.
  - Only update historical RTC variables on successful ACK decryption.
* Savings:
  - Suppresses LoRa transmission on ~85% of idle cycles.
  - Active measurement-only cycles complete in **~110 ms** (consuming **4.4 mA-s**).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. PHASE 4: DYNAMIC LORA POWER CONTROL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description:
  - Dynamically sets LoRa transmit power (10 dBm, 14 dBm, 20 dBm) depending on the RSSI of the previous received ACK.
  - Resets to maximum power (+20 dBm) on any failed or missed ACK.
* Savings:
  - Transmit current drops from 120 mA to **~28 mA** (at +10 dBm) when receiver is nearby.
